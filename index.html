PhD Candidate

Comparing and querying scientific workflows to enhance reuse

Supervisors

  • Sarah Cohen-Boulakia
  • Frédéric Lemoine

Summary

Over the past two decades, scientific workflows have become the preferred means of developing scientific analyses. In particular, they are designed to define highly reproducible analyses, while facilitating their development, execution, sharing and reuse. In recent times, workflow systems like Nextflow and Snakemake have emerged as valuable tools for bioinformaticians with programming expertise. These systems offer flexible scripting languages that enable swift development and execution, enhancing their usability. Therefore, a quickly growing number of workflows are available on online platforms such as Github and Gitlab, under a distributed and non annotated form, thus hard to query. In order to improve reuse and sharing of scientific workflows, this PhD thesis proposes to design a module to query them efficiently. To achieve this, the following tasks should be considered (1) Index existing workflows; (2) Annotate them by extracting interesting features; (3) Define similarity measures between workflows to compare them at different granularity levels; (4) Design a module capable of querying the indexed and annotated workflows, and using the previously defined similarity measures.
